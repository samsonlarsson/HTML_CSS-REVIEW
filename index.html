To catch a meal, a determined northern gannet slices into the waters off the island of Noss in Shetland, Scotland. The diving birds can break the water’s surface at 70 miles an hour and have nostrils that seal shut, allowing them to dive to depths up to 50 feet. “I took this picture … from a small boat using an underwater housing,” Your Shot photographer Felipe Foncueva explains, “surrounded by thousands of gannets flying, diving, and shouting around me.”

The first snow of the year paints this pine forest in Romania, and the bluish tint is reflected back off of Cuejdel Lake in this image by Your Shot photographer Gheorghe Popa.

While climbing a mountain to catch a sunrise in Nara, Japan, Your Shot photographer Yoshiki Fujiwara encountered this young male deer. In the Nara Prefecture of Japan, deer are free to roam—especially in Nara Park, where a herd of approximately 1,200 wanders freely and happily on the grounds of a temple. In the Shinto religion, deer are considered messengers of the gods—a reason they are so well protected. Your Shot photographer Mark Underwood spotted a woman carrying a mirror down the street in Havana, Cuba, and knew he had to scurry away from his friends to capture this shot. Reflected in the woman’s mirror is the back of an older car—a common sight in the Cuban capital, where classic American cars, many dating back to the 1950s, line the streets.
